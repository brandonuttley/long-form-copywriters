# Example Brief Template

Use this template when requesting a long-form copy draft. The more specific your inputs,
the stronger the output. Vague briefs produce vague copy.

Copy the template below, fill in your details, and paste it into Claude.

---

## Copy Brief

**Product / Service:**
What is being sold? Include: what it does, how it works, key features, backstory or origin,
anything that makes it different from alternatives.

> Example: A 12-week online strength training program for women over 40. Built around
> progressive overload with 3 sessions per week, 45 minutes each. Created by a physical
> therapist who noticed her patients kept getting injured following generic fitness content.
> No equipment required beyond dumbbells.

---

**Target Audience:**
Who is the reader? Include: who they are, what they've already tried, what they fear,
what they want, how aware they are of this type of solution.

> Example: Women 40–60 who are frustrated that fitness content isn't designed for their
> bodies or schedules. They've tried gym memberships they didn't use, YouTube workouts that
> felt condescending, and programs designed for 25-year-olds. They want to feel strong and
> capable, not thin. They're skeptical of anything that promises too much too fast.

---

**Desired Action:**
What should the reader do at the end of the copy?

> Example: Sign up for the program at $197. Enrollment closes Sunday.

---

**Format:**
What kind of copy is this?

Options:
- Sales letter (long-form, single page)
- Landing page (with sections and subheads)
- Email (single send or sequence — specify which)
- Advertorial (reads like an article)
- Direct mail letter
- VSL script (video sales letter)

> Example: Sales letter, long-form.

---

**Preferred Style (optional):**
Name a copywriter if you have one in mind. Leave blank and Claude will recommend one.

Options: Eugene Schwartz / Gary Halbert / David Ogilvy / Claude Hopkins / John Caples /
Joe Sugarman / Gary Bencivenga

> Example: Gary Bencivenga — the audience has been burned before and needs proof before
> they'll consider buying.

---

**Proof Points (optional but strongly recommended):**
List any specific facts, statistics, testimonials, credentials, or results you want included.
Specifics sell. The more you provide, the better the copy.

> Example:
> - 847 women have completed the program
> - Average result: 14% increase in grip strength over 12 weeks
> - 91% reported reduced joint pain by week 6
> - Created by Dr. Sarah Vance, DPT, 18 years in orthopedic physical therapy
> - Featured in Women's Health, March 2024
> - Testimonial: "I deadlifted my bodyweight for the first time at 52." — Karen M., Denver

---

**Tone Notes (optional):**
Anything to avoid or emphasize? Humor? Formality level? Language the audience uses?

> Example: The audience is smart and has heard every fitness promise before. Don't be
> breathless or hypey. They respond to competence and specificity, not enthusiasm.

---

## Minimal Brief (if you're short on time)

If you only have 2 minutes, Claude can work with this minimum:

```
Product: [one sentence]
Audience: [one sentence describing who they are and what they've tried]
Action: [what you want them to do]
Format: [sales letter / landing page / email / etc.]
```

The output will be stronger with a full brief, but this is enough to start.
